Animal Facts

Cheetah

A cheetah can run over 60 MPH for a short peroid of time...

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus
  • Average Length: 3 to 4 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 12 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is a species in the Felidae family of cats. ... The animal's common name is taken from "chita," a Hindi word meaning "spotted."

Elephant

Habitat loss is one of the key threats facing elephants.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Average Length: 18-21 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 45-70 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

Elephants, the largest land mammals, still face being killed for their tusks. Help WWF's elephant conservation efforts to fight poaching, conflict and habitat destruction.

Gazelle

There are 19 species of gazelle.

  • Scientific Name: Gazella
  • Average Length: 2- 4 feet
  • Average Speed: 60MPH
  • Habitat: African Savannah

Gazelle, any of several fleet, medium-sized antelopes with slender, evenly developed limbs, level backs, and long necks. Most gazelles are tan-coloured

Zebra

The plains zebra's range is fragmented, but spans much of southern and eastern Africa south of the Sahara

  • Scientific Name: Equus quagga
  • Average Length: 7 - 8 feet long
  • Average Lifespan: 20 to 30 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

Black and white stripes make the zebra one of the most recognizable animals in the world.

Giraffe

Giraffes are the world's tallest mammals, thanks to their towering legs and long necks.

  • Scientific Name: Giraffa
  • Average Height: 16 - 20 feet tall
  • Average Weight: 1800 - 2600 pounds
  • Habitat: African Savannah

Giraffes can eat 70-80 pounds of leaves a day and feed 16-20 hours

Rhino

The rhinoceros is Africa's armoured giant – like a tank on legs

  • Scientific Name: Rhinocerotidae
  • Average Height: 5.5-6 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 40-50 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

A rhinoceros, commonly abbreviated to rhino, is one of any five extant species of odd-toed ungulates in the family Rhinocerotidae, as well as any of the numerous extinct species.

Hippo

Hippos have unique skin that needs to be kept wet for a good part of the day.

  • Scientific Name: Hippopotamidae
  • Average Weight: 3,300 - 4000 pounds
  • Average Lifespan: 40 – 50 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

The common hippopotamus (Hippopotamus amphibius), or hippo, is a large, mostly herbivorous, semiaquatic mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa

Hyena

Hyenas or hyaenas are any feliform carnivoran mammals of the family Hyaenidae.

  • Scientific Name: Hyaenidae
  • Average Speed: 37 MPH
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 12 years
  • Habitat: African Savannah

It may look like a dog, but the spotted hyena is more closely related to cats, civets, and genets.
